"The Four Quartets is a series of four poems by <>T.S. Eliot</>, published individually from 1936 to 1942, and in book form in 1943; it was considered by <>Eliot</> himself to be his finest work. Each of the quartets has five movements and each is titled by a place name -- BURNT NORTON (1936), EAST COKER (1940), THE DRY SALVAGES (1941), and LITTLE GIDDING (1942)."
