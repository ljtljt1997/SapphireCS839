"Winesburg, Ohio (1919) is <>Sherwood Anderson</>'s masterpiece, a cycle of short stories concerning life in a small town at the end of the nineteenth century. At the center is <>George Willard</>, a young reporter who becomes the confidant of the town's solitary figures. Anderson's stories influenced countless American writers including <>Hemingway</>, <>Faulkner</>, <>Updike</>, <>Oates</> and <>Carver</>."
